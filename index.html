Where to go

Cultural & heritage sites

From fortress cities carved into rock to sacred tooth temples, Sri Lanka's ancient sites are among the most remarkable in Asia.

01
Sigiriya Rock Fortress

A 5th-century palace perched 200 metres above the jungle on a volcanic rock. Renowned for its mirror-wall inscriptions and cloud maiden frescoes.

UNESCO · Central Province
02
Anuradhapura

Sri Lanka's first ancient capital and a living pilgrimage city. Home to towering dagobas and the sacred Sri Maha Bodhi tree, planted in 288 BC.

UNESCO · North Central
03
Polonnaruwa

The medieval capital with stunning royal gardens, moonstone carvings, and the iconic Gal Vihara Buddha statues chiselled from a single granite face.

UNESCO · North Central
04
Temple of the Tooth, Kandy

Sri Lanka's most sacred Buddhist shrine, enshrining a relic of the Buddha's tooth. Each August it hosts the grand Esala Perahera procession.

UNESCO · Central Province
05
Adam's Peak (Sri Pada)

Sacred to Buddhists, Hindus, Muslims, and Christians — a pilgrimage mountain with a mysterious "sacred footprint" at its 2,243m summit.

Pilgrimage · Sabaragamuwa
06
Galle Fort

A 16th-century Dutch colonial fortified city on the southern coast, where cobblestone streets blend European ramparts with South Asian life.

UNESCO · Southern Province
Cultural Triangle tip: Follow the circuit linking Anuradhapura, Polonnaruwa, and Sigiriya for an immersive 3-day journey through Sri Lanka's ancient kingdoms.
A brief history

2,500 years in six moments

One of the world's oldest continuous civilisations, Sri Lanka has been shaped by Vedic princes, Buddhist missionaries, spice traders, and colonial empires.

543 BC
Legendary founding

Prince Vijaya arrives from northern India and founds the Sinhalese nation. The island appears in the ancient epic Ramayana as the kingdom of Lanka.

247 BC
Buddhism arrives

Emperor Ashoka's son Mahinda brings Buddhism to King Devanampiya Tissa. The sacred Bo Tree is planted in Anuradhapura — and still stands today.

5th century AD
Sigiriya built

King Kashyapa I constructs his fortress palace atop Sigiriya Rock, adorning its walls with the famous cloud maiden frescoes.

1505 – 1815
Colonial era

The Portuguese arrive and establish coastal control, followed by the Dutch in 1658 and the British in 1815, who finally unify the island under one rule.

1948
Independence

Ceylon gains independence from Britain. In 1972 the nation adopts the name Sri Lanka — meaning "Resplendent Island" in Sanskrit.

Today
A living heritage

Sri Lanka preserves 8 UNESCO World Heritage Sites while celebrating vibrant living traditions, ancient arts, and centuries-old pilgrimage routes.

What to experience

Living traditions

Sri Lanka's culture is not preserved behind glass — it breathes through festivals, art forms, and rituals practised every day.

🔥
Esala Perahera

Kandy's grand pageant — one of Asia's greatest spectacles, with decorated elephants, fire dancers, and costumed drummers parading through the city.

August · Kandy
🥁
Kandyan Dance

A centuries-old ceremonial art form featuring elaborate headdresses, acrobatic fire performances, and the driving beat of the geta beraya drum.

Performing Arts
🎨
Batik & Handloom

Traditional wax-resist textile art with vivid patterns unique to Sri Lanka. Artisan workshops in Kandy and Colombo welcome visitors year-round.

Crafts
🏮
Vesak Festival

The most sacred Buddhist holiday, marking the Buddha's birth and enlightenment. Streets are illuminated with enormous lanterns and pandols.

May · Island-wide
🍃
Tea Plantation Tours

Visit the misty Nuwara Eliya highlands to see Ceylon tea grown and processed — a heritage introduced by the British that Sri Lanka made its own.

Heritage · Hill Country
🌿
Ayurveda Traditions

A 3,000-year-old system of herbal medicine still practised daily. Authentic treatments and healing rituals are available across the island.

Wellness · Heritage
Dress respectfully: Cover shoulders and knees at temples. Remove footwear before entering any religious site, and walk clockwise around dagobas and shrines.
What to eat

Flavours of Sri Lanka

Sri Lankan cuisine is bold, coconut-rich, and deeply spiced — shaped by centuries of trade routes and multiple cultural influences.

01
Rice & Curry

The national dish — steamed rice surrounded by an array of curries, sambols, and chutneys, rich with coconut milk and fresh tempering.

02
Hoppers (Appa)

Bowl-shaped fermented rice-flour crepes served at breakfast with a cracked egg and spicy coconut sambol — crispy at the rim, soft at the base.

03
Ambul Thiyal

A sour-spiced dry fish curry using goraka (Malabar tamarind) — a coastal staple with centuries-old roots in the island's cooking traditions.

04
Kottu Roti

Chopped flatbread stir-fried with vegetables and egg on a hot griddle. The rhythmic clang of metal blades is a beloved sound of Sri Lankan street life.

05
Ceylon Tea

The world's most celebrated tea, grown in mist-shrouded highlands. Drink it the local way — strong, hot, and with condensed milk.

06
Kiri Bath

Coconut-milk rice cooked to a dense creamy cake, eaten on the first day of each month and at every major celebration. A taste of ritual.

☀️ Dec – Mar · Peak season

Dry and sunny on the west and south coasts. Ideal for the Cultural Triangle, Kandy, and beach visits. The busiest — and priciest — period.

🌧 May – Jul · SW Monsoon

Rains hit the west coast, but the east coast shines. Trincomalee and Arugam Bay are at their best with fewer crowds.

⭐ Aug – Sep · Shoulder season

Catch the Esala Perahera in August, enjoy moderate weather across the Cultural Triangle, and benefit from lower hotel rates.

Before you go

Essential travel tips

A few things to know before you land on the island.

Entry

Visa

Most nationalities need an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA). Apply at eta.gov.lk before departure for a 30-day tourist visa.

Money

Currency

Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR). ATMs are widely available in cities. Budget travellers can get by comfortably on USD 30–50 per day.

Transport

Getting around

The scenic Kandy–Ella train is a must. Tuk-tuks handle short trips; hire a driver for the Cultural Triangle circuit.

Communication

Language

Sinhala and Tamil are official. English is widely spoken in tourist areas, making communication easy throughout the island.

Health & Safety

Staying safe

Sri Lanka is safe for tourists. Drink bottled water, use sun protection in the highlands, and mind ocean currents on the coasts.

Customs

Etiquette

Never photograph Buddha statues with your back to them. Use your right hand when giving or receiving. Remove shoes at any religious site.
